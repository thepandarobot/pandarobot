*Pick your poison*, they say. And you, as a cultured gentleman, will pick the more obscure, more elite substance that eats through your guts, right?

A few years ago, I have abandoned IMDb for Letterboxd. I felt like the site died with the death of its forum section (as much of a toxic cesspool as it was), I couldn’t stand the feature articles promoting the lamest, middle of the road corporate product movies, all the lame celebrity photo collages that they kept reusing over and over, infantile fascination with superhero culture for a site that brands itself as *THE* global movie database where you supposedly find about ALL new releases, and worst of all, despite being my gateway to entry level cinema back in the day, the horrible, infantile, crayon-eating-stupid Top 250.

So I left, flipping off IMDb on my way out to Letterhead, the *superior* platform for real film connoisseurs. A24 and indie pictures featured galore on the what’s new  section? Don’t mind if I do! Real journalists in the review section? Yes please! 

Well, thing is… yeah, fast forward to this year and I did a U-turn back to IMDb. Turns out, Letterboxd sucks even harder.

Most of the features are locked behind a paywall, which I wouldn’t mind paying if it also allowed for a non-broken search function (how hard is it to implement a basic fuzzy search? Even this crusty ass site has one), it added a real TV section (some shows appear, but listed as 200h movies), and something that sadly not even the Letterboxd staff could fix: *THE GARBAGE, ABSOLUTELY UNFUNY JOKE REVIEWS*

It’s all just a bunch of thirsty teenagers providing no real commentary on the movie, salivating over some hot actor (I heard the word *actress* is nonsensical), engaging in bottom of the barrel fandom behavior which is ship culture, seeking out representation (an admirable goal in itself) by obnoxiously forcing it through said one line joke reviews that I have to inevitably filter out, and don’t even get me started on the toxic waste that is the review comment section. The IMDb boards had nothing on those zoomers throwing shit at each other like enraged monkeys.

I left and I don’t think I’ll look back, at least for a while. For the time being, I deactivated my brain and will check out if I’ll *recognize these celebrities by looking at their childhood red carpet pictures*. Fun.
