I cringe whenever I see one of those “OMG, so-and-so movie character is literally me”, especially when it’s a character that is openly a critique of their archetype and why you shouldn’t idolize them. Think all the characters that a Reddit bro likes, including but not limited to:

- Heath Ledger’s Joker
- Tyler Durden 
- Alex from A Clockwork Orange
- Travis Bickle
- That driver guy Ryan Reynolds played 
- Patrick Bateman
- Rorschach 
- Jordan Belfort
- etc

I was tempted to put Joaquin Phoenix’s Joker there too, but Todd Phillips implies everything the Joker did was justified in that movie somehow, which is the level of depth and insight to be expected from the director of The Hangover Trilogy and War Dogs, I guess… Oh, also Eren Yeager, Hajime Isayama’s Walter White wannabe turned accidental Adolf Hitler in an ending arc straight out of Mel Brooks’ The Producers.

![cringe](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/thepandarobot/pandarobot/main/_resources/B651BE26-C20F-4254-A0BD-ADCA77E01C7F.png)

I like to think that I’m better, but sadly, I too have a “literally me” moment, and it’s when I watch that creepy confession from P. T. Anderson’s *There Will Be Blood*:

 [![I hate people](https://img.youtube.com/vi/flz9WK3zl9A/0.jpg)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=flz9WK3zl9A)

I don’t agree with anything said there, but I know that deep down, no matter how much I talk myself into not feeling like that, I too see the worst in people. 

Whenever someone approached me, it couldn’t be for a simple “Hi!”, it was always “Hi, I noticed you’re very good at X, can you do that for me?” kind of shit. All interactions with me were always transactional and motivated by a “guess we gotta ask him” mentality.

I hated that, I still do, and I resented that I wasn’t worth the value of a simple, unmotivated interaction. I was just a means to an end. That motivated me to not help out people deliberately, even when it was something incredibly easy for me to do, or charge money whenever I could. If it’s transactional, at least let’s make it fair!

That’s quite the toxic way to interact with people, and I’m still in the process of fixing that part of myself, but can I really be blamed for taking that path in the first place? I do see the worst in people, because nobody ever extended the olive branch. It’s quite hilarious how much people seemed to dislike me almost instantly, and I went through various phases of different “personalities” through my adolescence, figuring myself out, thinking one of them will click with people (none of them did, and it was exhausting to keep an act for so long, so I dropped that shit when I stopped caring so much)

I don’t like Daniel Plainview, and I think it’s stupid to model yourself after him. P.T.A. wrote him as a cautionary tale. Spoilers, but at the end, he is all alone, devoid of family, friends, even of his chance for another friendly adversarial adventure with Paul Dano’s character. But no, that guy is human repeller in the purest form. And I don’t want to end up like him.

He’s literally me, but… you know, I’m working on it.
