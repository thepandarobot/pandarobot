I am extremely interested in Transhumanism. It is one of my fascinations with the future that I can’t seem to ever fully satisfy. (Side note, anyone read All Tommorrows by C. M. Kosemen? If not, do yourself a favour and check it out, it floats around the internet as a free PDF since 2006)

I am also interested in weird, moody, arthouse anime. My posts about Neon Genesis Evangelion should have made that clear already.

In a Ven Diagram, what would be at the intersection? Well, Serial Experiments Lain, for once. SEL was released in 1998, right as the internet was starting to grow from baby to toddler and the anime industry was emboldened to go into weirder, more experimental directions thanks to the success of Evangelion (like… *saved the industry* success, no joke). It depicts the story of Lain Iwakura, an introverted fourteen year old who gets gifted a computer and starts to develop a second personality that seems to inhabit the dark corners of the digital world. I won’t go into plot details to save prospective viewers from spoilers and spare old fans from a hack job of a synopsis. Instead, I’ll paste the opening below, set to Bôa’s Duvet, and trust me when I say that it perfectly incapsulates the show (both the vibe and the borderline-incoherence of the surreal narative).

 [![Boa Duvet Lain](https://img.youtube.com/vi/MM8RufZr5lw/0.jpg)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MM8RufZr5lw)

*also check out Archipel’s video on Yoshitoshi ABe, the visual artist that worked on the anime and the videogame*


Thing is, there’s a website in the show called the Wired. Not the American one with reviews and crap, a proto-MySpace-slash-digital-world where most people log in to communicate and daze in front of a blue-radiating monitor.

Well, turns out that some fan(s) created a real world version of that site, accessible from the retro, cyberpunk, synth wave-themes website Neocities. And you apparently need an invite to get there.

I joined the Discord server and I’m gonna try to get an invite, but even if it fails, the community present there seems to be pretty chill and willing to share interesting and eerie shit that I like, so nothing was lost. Fingers crossed I’ll get an invite, though.

Oh, and also, watch (or rewatch) Serial Experiments Lain. For a ’98 anime about the parasocial future of mankind in the social networking age, it got so much right it makes me wonder if John Titor was a writer on this.
