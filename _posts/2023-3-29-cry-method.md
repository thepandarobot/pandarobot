Reading a Harvard article (well, the blurb that Google Search selected for me), I found out that, quote, “crying releases oxytocin and endogenous opioids, also known as endorphins. These feel-good chemicals help ease both physical and emotional pain.”

Now, I’m no Daniel Day Lewis. I can’t cry on cue, and as any self-respecting insecure man, I seldom cry unless watching some really good tear-jerker, or skunk drunk and listening to sad music.

The problem with the first approach is that, the more you watch the tear-jerker, the less you cry (I watched The Elephant Man exactly 1.5 times and I’m afraid to ever get numb to it). But the second one? Hmmm…

I watched a lot of manly men cry while drunk, and the very next day, they treated it like “yeah bro, I was so wasted last night, like what was that? crying like a bitch, lololol 🤙”. Well, we know from Robert Caro that power doesn’t corrupt, but it merely reveals. I’d like to add alcohol to that list as well.

For the past year, and going out of the dreaded COVID-19 pandemic with a more distant society (even for my taste, monkey’s paw I guess), I have been experimenting with this monthly cure of tears and alcohol. Now, before we go any further, I’d like to emphasize that regular consumption of alcohol is a slippery slope toward addiction, and one should not treat it recreationally if it becomes a daily habit.

The recipe is quite simple:
Step 1: get drunk, preferably while watching some funny videos or action scenes, for contrast with the later feeling.
Step 2: when the alcohol kicks in (might take a while, sadly for me I have quite the tolerance and delay in getting drunk), you will inevitably gravitate toward moody music videos with lyrics that will seem incredibly deep for you (depending on the alcohol intake, it might even be a cat food commercial). Alternatively, you can keep a stash of sad videos on hand, but watch them on rotation to prevent the dreaded numbness.

That’s it. Have a good cry, then wake up next morning with puffy eyes, but feeling fresh on the inside, clean like a baby. Jerking off the heart, I call it, and it’s the true antidote to the modern times (suck it, Jordan Peterson, you hateful prick)
