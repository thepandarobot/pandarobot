I started with Astrid's character design based from my notes I wrote trying to figure out the character, and all I wrote at that point was 'pink tears' and 'rose crown'. I like to develop the backstory and the visual design simultaneously so that I can picture the character when I write it. One major breaktrough I had in developing the visual language of BANANA CIDER actually came by doing this design: the colored outlines. I experimented with colored outlines here and instantly fell in love with them. They are way more expressive, they fit the tone of the story perfectly, and have a tendency to hide mistakes and create a nice texture. In fact, I gave up completely on the sketch layer for this series and decided to sketch directly on the inks layer, erasing most of the guide lines but leaving some of them (which I feel gives a nice texture and personality to the drawings).
