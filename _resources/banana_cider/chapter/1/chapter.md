# May you never forget me


<img width="500vw" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/thepandarobot/pandarobot/main/_resources/banana_cider/illustrations/1.PNG"/>

Foamy waves kept violently crashing over a shore, ebbing and flowing, their sound drowning out the cries of seagulls in the distance. Near the horizon, the deep blue-green sea was glistening in the sun, underneath a pale blue sky. With each wave, small seashell shards and pieces of kelp washed over the clay-like strip of wet sand.

A girl in a pale pink nightgown lay face down on the sand, her auburn hair, wet and full of sand, covering her face. Her nightgown was sticking to her body like a second skin, soaked by the waves that kept washing over her. She wore a crown of red roses over her head, and her skin was pale, so much so that blue veins were visible on the back of her hands.

She turned her head to the right, coarse sand crumbling off her cheek like a mask. Her eyes still closed, she has a round, youthful face, no older than sixteen. Light pierced through her eyelid, a burning orange, then a blurry, horizontal white line formed as her eyelashes parted and her vision focused.

A beautiful eye was in full view, emerald green with a steely gray kernel around the iris. It caught every speck of light available, its surface kept constantly slick by a never-ending flow of pink tears.

Her vision focused, now becoming a ninety degree flipped view of a shore: a barren stretch of sand and beach grass to the South, with what appeared to be skeletal ruins in the distance, and a sharp vertical line through the middle, separating the ocean on the left, and the pale blue sky on the right.

The girl finally lifted her head with great effort, turning her view of the landscape back to its natural state, but receiving a sharp sensation in her abdomen in response.

She groaned and attempted to support herself on her elbows, but couldn't. Another cold wave washed over her, and red ribbons swirled through the retreating water like drops of ink.

\<\< What is this sensation? \>\>, she wondered.

\<PAIN\>

Sharp pain in the abdomen and a scorching burn in the guts, both alternating and creating an unpredictable symphony that couldn't be tuned out with time.

Bending herself in a praying position, then lifting her torso up until her knees dig deep in the wet sand and her bum rested on her heels, the girl examined the front of her pink nightgown. She stuck her finger through the cut in the fabric, outlined by faded red-brown spots, and reached a fresh wound, a long cut above her pelvic bone, sore all around and still oozing thick, syrupy blood. A crust of black blood and sand formed while she lay on her abdomen, protecting the wound from the salt water that could have sutured it.

The girl whimpered and tried to press the skin around the cut, but even the lightest touch was met with a new, sharper pain in response.

Collecting all her strength, she got up on her bare feet, dizzy, zigzagging across the shore while the waves gradually filled her footprints with water and sand, slowly erasing them behind her.

Her mind could not process all the information coming her way, so she focused on one thing at a time.

\<\< So bright. The sky, it feels like I'm staring at a lightbulb \>\>

\<\< How did I get here anyway? \>\>

\<\< I must have lost a lot of blood. I think there was something you could do to stop the bleeding, but… no, wait, that's only on arms and legs \>\>

\<\< This nightgown isn't mine, is it? I like the color, maybe it is \>\>

\<\< Where was I going anyway? \>\>

Finally, two large, blurry, rectangular silhouettes appeared in the distance, a temporary objective for the aimless girl. She dragged her feet across the sand, her toes digging into it as she could barely lift her feet. With each step, the silhouettes grew clearer and clearer, yet still dark, despite the Sun being right above, vanishing any shadow.

Two figures of armor wrapped in cloth, one light, one dark, both bearing metal helmets resembling plague doctor masks, hats on top of their helmets. The glass discs that formed the dark figure's eyes were ruby red, while the light one's were navy blue, both pairs reflecting the girl's silhouette. Behind each of the seven foot figure was a replica of _Rodin's Gates of Hell_, lying in the sand, nothing behind either one.

After overcoming her paralyzing fear, the girl meekly approached the dark armour, looking up at the towering figure and extending her shaking hand, palm trembling and ready to connect with the cold metal.

"Those are Sentinels", a voice coming from behind suddenly said.

Startled, the girl managed to turn her head, looking over the shoulder at a dark haired girl in an orange pullover, holding a glass bottle in one hand and a silver coin in the other.

She must have been in her mid-twenties, short, with a pixie haircut, bangs, ears exposed, each pierced several times up the cartilage. She had slanted eyes with thick eyelashes, moss green with a light brown core, an upturned nose, and her mouth was locked into a smirk. She had a white shirt underneath her pullover, with a lime green bowtie, and high waisted black pants with black sneakers on her feet.

The young woman used the silver coin to skillfully remove the cap off the bottle with a 'tssss-' sizzle of gas escaping, prompting bubbled to form in the pink beverage inside. She took a big gulp, finishing the drink in seconds, then wiped her mouth with the back of her hand and threw the empty bottle so far away it vanished.

Insitanly turning her gaze back to the girl in the nightgown, in a move that was insect-like, praying mantis-like, she pointed the index finger of each hand toward both armours, the various bracelets on her wrists jiggling.

"One of them always tells the truth, the other always lies", she continued. "You get to ask either of them one question, and if you get it right, you can pass through the right gate"

"Right gate?", the young girl asked, confused. "Pass to… where?"

"Beats me. I wasted my question a long time ago"

"What is this place, who are you?"

"Both excellent questions! My name's Evelyn. This one's free, all other answers I will provide for a small fee", Evelyn replied, turning her smirk into a sly smile and gesturing toward her open hand, ready to receive payment.

"I… umm… my name's Astrid, and I… I don't have any money", Astrid meekly said, with almost cracking voice.

They awkwardly stared at each other for a good ten seconds before Evelyn gave her a military salute and did a heel turn.

"Okay, then, have a good one!"

"Wait! I'm bleeding, can you help me? Please!"

"Sorry, but that doesn't sound like something I can do for free", Evelyn replied without turning around.

She started walking away before freezing in place, one feet up, then she did another heel turn and rushed toward Astrid, getting really in her face to the point it made the young girl uncomfortable.

"What's that thing? It keeps flowing outta your eyes"

"Ummm... Tears?"

Evelyn touched her cheek and looks at the tips of her fingers, examining the strange liquid.

"Nah, that can't be!", she stated. "These are pink"

Without giving it a second thought, Evelyn stuck her finger in her mouth and gave the tear a taste-test, looking up, left and right as if trying to decode the notes of a wine or a fine food.

"Yuck... Salty. Yep, they're tears, alright", she concluded, much to Astrid's confusion. "That cut must hurt like hell, with these tears that don't seem to stop flowing"

"It does, actually. It burns like hell too!"

Evelyn did a sly smile, intrigued.

"You're interesting, Astrid, I'll give you that! I'll nurse you back to health, like a baby bird that fell outta nest, and when you're back on your feet, I'll collect the fee. Sounds fair?"

Astrid looked confused, but then she smiled and extended her hand. The two girls shook hands.

"How old are you, anyway?", Evelyn asked.

"Sixteen. You?"

"Come on, we got a long way to go to the Supermarket and we might lose our window"

"Supermarket? But we're in the middle of nowhere"

Evelyn snapped her heels together and a lightning-like glow flashed on both her feet, light with morphed into a pair of orange and green rollerblades. Tiny ribbons of steam rose from the rollerblades and curled up in the air.

"Go on, do yours!", Evelyn prompted.

Astrid looked confused.

"I… I don't… What?"

Evelyn groaned.

"Oh, boy, no Lightning Rollerblades either? Guess we gotta walk, then…"

"Sorry...", Astrid instinctively appoligized, even if she didn't fully comprehend what for.

Evelyn took one of Astrid's arms and put it behind her neck, helping the girl walk through all her pain. As they walked further and further away, Astrid kept peeking over her shoulder, looking at the two gates and the two Sentinels, slowly shirking into the distance.

A gentle breeze passed through, bending pale yellow beachgrass blades that grew here and there. To the right, the skeletal ruins Astrid saw earlier came into full view: ruins of buildings, some tilted to one side, crumbling, devoid of windows, hanging underneath a web of cables that still clung to lopsided poles. To the left, the sea kept ebbing and flowing, keeping the sand wet.

"I bet you accept all you see because it feels like a dream, right?", Evelyn suddenly asked, receiving a nod in return from Astrid. "Yeah, that's what I like about this place. No need to bake your noodle over unimportant stuff. Just go with the flow"

After a while, a mighty obstacle showed up in the path of their journey: an overpass section, collapsed right in front of them, the lines on the asphalt still visible. Astrid looked dejected, almost losing all remaining strength in her body.

"Oh, no... We walked all this way for nothing!"

"Don't be so gloomy, crybaby girl! Just watch and learn!"

Evelyn removed Astrid's arm from her nape, then flicked her coin up, letting it spin in the air a multitude of times, before catching in with a swift motion. She kept it firmly between her thumbnail and index finger, closing one eye and aiming at the overpass section. Then, she flicked...

\<VRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR\>

The coin launched with great speed and vanished from sight before reaching the overpass, but its moment of impact was clear: a spherical crater formed in the asphalt, and a split-second later it got replaced by a circular hole, chunks of asphalt, concrete and metal crumbling down. Through the same hole, the coin returned like a boomerang, caught by Evelyn with yet another swift and ample arm motion.

Astrid looked in disbelief, unable to process what she was seeing.

\<\< Have I lost that much blood that my mind is playing tricks on me? \>\>

\<\< Can't worry about that now... Maybe it's a dream... \>\>

\<\< Still... the curiosity proved too great not to ask \>\>

"How did you do that?"

Evelyn simply closed her eyes, put one hand behind her ear and leaned toward Astrid, extending the other hand.

"What's that? Sounds like free talking to me. I gotta open a tab"

Astrid pouted and crossed her arms. Evelyn took one arm and placed it back over her neck.

Later...

Sun was setting over the shore and the two found themselves in front of an old, dilapidated supermarket, the parking lot slowly blending into the sand. Orange light gave everything a warm tint, casting long shadows behind the two. The seagulls became restless, their cries getting louder and louder.

"We only get a one hour window during Golden Hour; that's when the doors are unlocked", Evelyn explained while she put a coin in the anti-theft lock of a shopping cart, freeing it from the array of other carts. "We need to get in and out within that hour, otherwise we'll be locked inside. From outside, twenty-four hours pass between each window"

"And from in— you know what, don't even bother!", Astrid caught herself before spewing out a destined-to-be-ignored question.

"You catch on fast, Astrid!"

The girls walk in, Astrid pushing the shopping cart while also using it to support herself.

Endless tall shelves stretched on both sides of a long aisle.

"I'll tell you this upfront: the Sentinels normally leave you alone. Their job is to make sure that you don't leave Arcadia", Evelyn explained, walking with her hands in the pockets of her pants.

"Arcadia?", Astrid instinctively asked.

"But YOU can't leave them alone, Astrid. When you destroy a Sentinel, it leaves behind a coin. And you need coins to pay for stuff. And pay me back, of course"

"Wait, you want me to fight those things? Forget it!"

"Oh, don't worry! I'll whip you up into a mean, lean, Sentinel-crushing machine!"

"Thanks, Evelyn… But I'm more concerned about my bleeding wound for now"

"Yeah, yeah…", Evelyn said while doing a dismissive hand wave. "We'll get that fixed in no time with a gulp of Banana Cider"

\<\< Banana Cider? \>\>

They both soon find themselves at the end of the aisle, in front of a very large vending machine. Evelyn put in a coin, pressed a button, then watched as a cider bottle fell. She used another one of her coins to open the cap.

"Bottoms up! And don't worry, there's no alcohol! It's just that you may find yourself suffering from light amnesia, that's all!"

"Wait, what?", Astrid asked, shocked.

"Minor side effect. Well, not that you remember much to begin with, am I right?". She rubbed her chin. "Maybe you drank this before? Who knows..."

Astrid took a cautious sniff, wrinkling her small nose, and her reaction was pleasantly surprised.

\<\< It smells sweet! \>\>

She took a gulp, then dropped to her knees in pain, clutching her stomach. The cider bottle shattered on the floor.

"Cleanup on aisle one!", Evelyn yelled, visibly amused at the sight of Astrid's pain.

"It burns! It burns even worse than before!", Astrid yelled out in pain, the flow of her tears growing larger.

"That's okay, let it out, let it aaaaaaall out!"

Evelyn put her hand on Astrid's forehead, as if to help her vomit.

"Oh, boy, I should've known when you said you got them burns"

"It burns!", Astrid yelled in pain, almost losing her voice.

Astrid's neck and cheeks got a red-orange glow, almost like shining a light through amber. Steam started to escape through her parted lips.

"I know, I know! Let it all out!"

"It buuuuuurns!"

While saying the last word, orange flames with a thick white core escaped from her mouth like from a furnace, forming a thick pillar of fire that Evelyn directed toward the tall ceiling by pulling on Astrid's forehead.

After a good ten seconds, the fire died out, then the ribbon of white smoke curled up and faded out, and finally Astrid's body went limp, supported by Evelyn like a reproduction of _Pietà_.

Through the slashed fabric, the bleeding cut in her skin slowly sealed itself, sticking back together and leaving only a crust of dried blood and sand cover her fair skin. No scar was left behind.

After a few seconds, Astrid's vision focused, and the towering silhouette over her became Evelyn, looking down at her with a bright smile.

Astrid gasped for air and wheezed a few times, as if she was a newborn for the first time outside the womb. She put her hands on her cheeks, and much to her surprise, the tears were still flowing.

\<\< There's still pain \>\>

\<\< But... it's different now \>\>

\<\< I don't feel it in my abdomen, and the burns are gone \>\>

\<\< So why... \>\>

"Guess we couldn't get the waterworks to stop, huh?", Evelyn said, raising Astrid by the armpits before dropping her like a sack of dirt.

"Aw! Why did you do that?"

"Stop playing the princess and walk by yourself!"

Astrid got up, ripped a strip from her nightgown and wrapped in over the cut like a shawl around her waist, covering the bloody cut. The two then resumed their walk through the aisles, Astrid still pushing the cart.

"Oh, man, a firebreather! Jeaaalous! All I got is this crummy coin flick, but I worked hard on it!", Evelyn said while looking around, walking with her hands cupped behind her back. "You'll learn to melt them ugly bastards in no time!"

"Strange… the burn is all gone"

"Hmm? I heard the fire is ignited by emotion. Maybe you need to get angry or something"

"That sounds awful!"

"Yeah, whatever, let's get you a pair of rollerblades and bounce before closing time!"

Finally, they arrive in front of an endless shelf full of colorful rollerblade boxes of different sizes.

"We need to move fast, the cashier here is so slow!", Evelyn noted. "Pick your size and color, but don't take too long"

"Oh… right!"

Evelyn paced around, impatient, doing coin tricks on one hand and waiting while Astrid tried on a bunch of rollerblades.

"Bet it's gonna be pink", she muttered to herself.

Right as she said that, Astrid rolled toward her with a big, warm smile on her face, stopping with a little spin and her arms spread wide.

"What do you think?", Astrid asked.

Evelyn looked down at the pink-as-candy rollerblades and smiled.

"Beautiful! Now let's go to the checkout before closing hour"

They head toward the cash registers, an endless row of them but only one worked.

"How much do I owe you yet?", Astrid asked.

"Let's see… one cider, rollerblades, bunch of stupid questions…"

Astrid looked at her with a sad and desperate look on her face.

"Can't you cut me some slack on the questions? You haven't even answered any of them"

"You think money grows on trees? Must be a spoiled only child". Suddenly, she spotted the only cash register worked. "Oh, there's no line, let's go! Go go go go!"

They dashed to the register. A few minutes later, Evelyn was waiting with arms crossed and Astrid meekly watched as the comically long band slowly moved the rollerblades toward the scanner. Their eyes followed the rollerblades, and after a while, Evelyn tried to push them further.

"No touching the products on the band!", yelled the cashier, a chubby old lady with a nasal voice and a Domino mask on top of which she wore glasses with straps that kept dangling down to her second chin.

Evelyn looked even more pissed. The cashier slowly scanned the rollerblades one by one, taking a few tries on each one.

"One coin", the cashier finally said.

Evelyn tossed her a coin, grabbed the rollerblades and Astrid's wrist, and rushes out.

"Keep the change!", Evelyn yelled.

"There's no change!", the cashier yelled back. "Cheapskate"

As they bolted toward the door, Evelyn snapped her heels, making her rollerblades appear. She took Astrid in her arms and they roll out as the doors narrowly close behind them. With two impulse kicks, Evelyn got incredible speed, proving that 'Lightning Rollerblades' deserved their name. Astrid looked to the side, her hair fluttering to the back of her head, watching all the cash registers wheeze by and blend in a blur.

The metal doors slowly started to close, narrowing the strip of orange light that poured from outside.

"We're not gonna make it!", Astrid yelled.

With one more impulse kick, Evelyn got the last bit of speed that allowed the two of them to slide through the doors as they slammed shut with a loud bang behind them.

Evelyn did a T-stop and stopped in place in the parking lot, and a few seconds of disbelief washed over them. Suddenly, they both started laughing. Evelyn then dropped Astrid and her rollerblades on the ground.

"Well… that was fun!"

"Oh, yeah…", Astrid said, rubbing her back. "I think my backbone is busted"

"I'll come to collect my debt later. I don't wanna act all 'loan shark' on you, so make some coins and we'll stay friends, ok?"

She extended her hand toward Astrid. Astrid smiled, took her hand, and let Evelyn help her up.

"Sure!"

Evelyn gave Astrid a wink, then rolled away. Astrid watches her as the sun set, turning the sky orange and pink around the horizon, and revealing a few purple clouds.

\<\< I woke up in this strange new world called Arcadia, and it all seems like a dream with lots of rules that I yet don't understand \>\>

\<\< I don't remember most things about how I got here, or how my life was before, but I find one thing to comfort me in all of this: \>\>

\<\< I got at least one friend here \>\>
