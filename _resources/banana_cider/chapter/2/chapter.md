# 2. THE TREE OF LIFE

<img width="500vw" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/thepandarobot/pandarobot/main/_resources/banana_cider/illustrations/2.PNG"/>

Astrid and Evelyn rollerblade up a steep hill, racing each other. The road was sinuous, twisting left and right and forcing the girls to get their knees close to the asphalt when attacking the corners.

"I'm winning! I'm winning!", Evelyn yelled, leading the race.

"Of course you are, you tripped me twice", Astrid retorted, her knees full of scraps

"Shut up, sourpuss! You're about to see something cool!"

When they reached the top and stopped, the blinding light of the morning sun, obscured by the hill they climbed, blinded Astrid for a second, making her shield her eyes.

After her eyes adjusted and her vision focused, she remained stunned for a minute at the wonderful sight: a large field, full of purple plants, with a river twisting down the middle, and a large, secular tree in the distance, with giant orange fruit hanging from its branches. Astrid couldn't help but gasp and cover her mouth.

"It's beautiful!", she whispered.

"Takes my breath away every time", Evelyn said. "The Strawberry Fields. And that right there in the distance, that's the Tree of Life"

"The Tree of Life…"

"It bears some strange fruit. But what's more interesting is that the Sentinels that patrol that place are less dangerous and easier to beat than the rest"

"Why?"

"You'll see…"

They rolled down at great speed, reaching the point where asphalt crumbled and turned into dirt. Purple strawberry leaves, covered in dew, grazed by their legs, keeping the air humid and cool. The dirt road followed the small and clear river that ran down the field, glistening in the sun and making a quiet noise.

As they got closer to the Tree of Life, details on the strange fruits started to fade in: tiny red veins streaked the orange, translucent skin, almost like a placenta, and silhouettes of figures in fetal position appeared when light shone through.

"Those are people in there", Astrid exclaimed, as the two crawled to a halt.

"The Tree of Life is the entry point to Arcadia. Everybody here got by getting ripe and falling from up there and into the river. This information will be billed for 59.99"

"Amazing! Except for the last part"

The ground suddenly started to shake and the fruits started to dangle. Astrid looked around, scared, while Evelyn gestured for her to stay quiet and observe. Suddenly, a light Sentinel emerges from behind the gargantuan trunk, carrying a giant spiked mace in one hand. Its head turned left and right, observing the two intruders, then he slammed the mace to the ground, spiked end into dirt, and rested both hands on the end of the handle.

"Is it going to attack us?", Evelyn asked.

"Not unprovoked, no. He's here to defend the people in the fruits"

"Then let's not provoke him!"

Wearing a mischievous smile, Evelyn reached to her back and retrieved a bottle of Banana Cider, cold condensation drops sliding down the curved glass. She sneaked behind Astrid and touched the bottle of the glass to her nape.

"EEK!"

\<\< EEK! \>\>

"Come on, firehose! Drink up, melt that bastard, and earn your beverage", Evelyn said, opening up the bottle for Astrid.

"Nu-uh!"

"Oh, don't make me twist your arm until you comply! Besides, I'm here to help you, but you gotta do some work yourself!". She turned around to hide her face as she couldn't keep herself from smiling. "Or do you think you can make it by yourself here?"

Astrid started fidgeting, worried, her flow of tears getting larger by the second.

"No, don't get mad, Evelyn, I'll… I'll do it"

Evelyn turned around and handed her the drink. Astrid finished it in five gulps, then gave Evelyn the empty bottle, only to have it tossed away. She pressed her palm on her abdomen, feeling the burning sensation from before, only not as strong.

"I don't think the fire's coming out!"

"That's cause your sorry for that sonovabitch! Don't! It's an empty suit, it's not alive!"

"But he's guarding the people, you said so yourself!"

"Do you feel sorry for a gate because it's guarding your house? Just go there and smelt him!"

Astrid meekly approached the Sentinel, her gaze lifting higher and higher as she got closer. The iron giant was frozen. The cloth over his armor gently moved as a breeze blew by and rustled the strawberry plants and the tree leaves.

Astrid frowned, the feeling of guilt starting to light up the embers in her stomach. Suddenly, the pain turned sharp, but she did her best to stay on her feet, trembling and making a muzzle with her left hand, keeping it in front of her mouth.

The Sentinel's head moved down with a metallic screech, both helmet lenses reflecting the trembling girl. He suddenly raised the mace, Astrid finding herself in its shadow, paralyzed with fear.

She opened her mouth, but nothing came out, not even a sound. She puckered her lips and blew out nothing but hot air, struggling so hard that only a quiet whistle emerged.

The Sentinel readied its attack and all hope seemed lost.

\<\< Please! \>\>

Suddenly, a coin embedded in the chest plate, creating a concave indent before flinging back into Evelyn's hand. The Sentinel rose its head, but the second attack struck its helmet, creating a slot that burned orange around the edges.

The coin then passed in and out through the helmet with great speed, turning it into Swiss cheese, until finally, the entire armor cracked like glass and shattered, and two coins returned into Evelyn's palm.

Still shaking and whimpering, Astrid flinched when Evelyn patted her on the head.

"It's ok, you're a lover, not a fighter", she declared to the scared girl. "But I'm a fighter, not a pimp, so I'll have to make you learn the ropes if I want to make you useful"

"I'm s-s-sorry!"

"Do you like Ice Cream?"

"Huh?"

Evelyn ripped a strawberry plant from the dirt, roots and all, and looked at the fruit.

"I myself like it chocolate flavored and topped with fresh fruit"

"I, umm…". Astrid composed herself. "I like vanilla"

"Of course you do"

"Where do we get Ice Cream"

"The Supermarket, of course!"

Just then, a fruit right above their heads started to dangle. Evelyn pushed Astrid away and they watched as the fruit fell, ripping apart like a water balloon, pink liquid pouring out. A naked girl, no older than Astrid, started coughing and wheezing, gasping for air, the pink slime sliding down her ribs, breasts, arms, neck.

"Oh, God! It's another girl!", Astrid exclaimed.

As if guided by something, without opening her eyes, the girl crawled on all fours toward the river.

"Watch o—", Astrid yelled, but Evelyn stopped her.

"What are you doing?"

"She's gonna drown!"

"What did I just tell you earlier? She ain't gonna drown, she's gonna end up on that shore as you did"

"Wait…"

\<\< But why did I wake up with that wound? \>\>

"What?", Evelyn asked.

Astrid realized that a question like that will not have an answer, but uttering it at that moment, when she knew so little, was not the best course of action.

"We… we only have an hour to buy ice cream, right?"

Evelyn cracked an even wider smile.

"I'm starting to like you more and more, Astrid! Come, it's my treat, but you'll have to pay me back later"

"Of course!"

"What do you mean, 'of course'?"

The two laughed as they rolled away back to the hill, and from somewhere in the distance, a new Sentinel emerged to replace its fallen comrade.
